About Geobadges

There's never been such a good time to learn about our changing world. But our systems for promoting geographic competence have not kept pace. Too often students are confined to old fashioned tests, paper maps, or if they are lucky, mapping applications they can only access on computers at school. The GeoBadges initiative provides a new open learning ecosystem focused on rewarding skill building and acts of service that students - of all ages - do in the geography domain. We work with government agencies, companies, educational institutions and nonprofit groups who design and submit GeoBadges for review by our Peer Review Committee. Once accepted, they are opened for anyone to work on and earn. Earning is handled through the Credly.com platform. Points of Contact at the host organization of the GeoBadges are responsible for issuing them to requesting learners. If you're an individual with a great idea for a GeoBadge you want to manage yourself, or have another organization adopt, you're also welcome to submit your idea for consideration by the Peer Review Committee. GeoBadges complies with the COPPA and DCMA laws of the United States. Only individuals age 13+ are eligible to earn GeoBadges. If any content on the site seems inappropriate or out of compliance, please contact us immediately at geobadges@americangeo.org. To learn more or to support the GeoBadges effort, contact: Selene Lawrence Communications & Outreach Manager American Geographical Society lawrence@americangeo.org (917) 745-8354

About the Peer Review Committee

The Peer Review Committee is a volunteer group of experts organized by AGS that is responsible for approving new badges added to the GeoBadges.org platform. MapStory

MapStory is the atlas of change that everyone can edit. It is a community, not a company, that envisions a world where everyone's knowledge can be tapped, peer reviewed, organized and shared so that we all can dramatically improve our understanding of the rich past, complex present and uncertain future we share as living beings on planet Earth.

GeoMakers

GeoMakers is an open community that collaborates to dream, build and implement open source maker projects that involve mapping, remote sensing, navigating and understanding our world geographically. To learn more, visit geomakers.org.

Open Geospatial Consortium

The OGC (Open Geospatial Consortium) is an international not for profit organization committed to making quality open standards for the global geospatial community. These standards are made through a consensus process and are freely available for anyone to use to improve sharing of the world's geospatial data. To learn more, visit opengeospatial.org

TeachOSM

TeachOSM is an online resource to assist educators at all levels to introduce open source mapping using the OpenStreetMap platform in the classroom. To learn more, visit teachosm.org

Peace Corps

International Network of Crisis Mappers

The International Network of Crisis Mappers (Crisis Mappers Net) is the largest and most active international community of experts, practitioners, policymakers, technologists, researchers, journalists, scholars, hackers and skilled volunteers engaged at the intersection of humanitarian crises, new technology, crowd-sourcing, and crisis mapping. To learn more, visit crisismappers.net

GeoPlunge

GeoPlunge is an award-winning game developed by LearningPlunge, LLC based in Washington DC. It teaches children communication skills and sportsmanship, in addition to social studies and spatial knowledge. GeoPlunge games are easy to learn and can be played at home, in school, or at one of the tournaments organized by LearningPlunge, Inc.
